Setting up the game

  1. Shuffle the Venue deck and deal one Venue card face up in the top left corner of the play area. Set the rest of the Venue deck aside (it will be used again in later rounds).
  2. Put the Round 1 token to the left of the face-up Venue card. Set the Round 2 and Round 3 tokens aside (they will be used, not surprisingly, in later rounds).
  3. Place the six Group labels (A through F) in a vertical line below the Venue card.
  4. In a small stack to the right of each Group label, put the three corresponding Round 1 Content tokens corresponding to the Group, face up in order from highest 1st place payoff (6) on the top to lowest (3) on the bottom. Set aside the remaining Content tokens (they will be used in later rounds).
  5. Shuffle the A deck and the B deck. To the left of each Group label, deal an A card and a B card face up to form an a cappella group (12 cards forming 6 groups in total). Set the rest of the A and B decks aside (they will be used again in later rounds).
  6. Shuffle the Judge deck. Deal one Judge card face up to the right of the Venue card; then deal four more Judge cards face down in a row extending to the right. Set the rest of the Judge deck aside (it will be used again in later rounds).
  7. Place the Performance dice in a pile in the middle of the table (they will be used at the end of each round).
  8. Place the Hits tokens (eighth notes, quarter notes, half notes, whole notes) where players can reach them. Give each player 8 Hits to start the game (suggested breakdown: 1 half note, 1 quarter note, and 2 eighth notes).
  9. Place the seven pairs of Share tokens near the Hits tokens.
  10. Shuffle the Unexpected Change deck and deal two Unexpected Change cards face down to each player (who can look at their own cards anytime). Put the remaining deck near the Hits tokens where players can reach it.
  11. Hand out the reference sheets so that all players have access to one.
  12. Give the Downbeat token to the player who has most recently been on a stage. They will take the first action of the game.

End-of-round judging

First, set aside all Content tokens and Share tokens that were not claimed during the round.

For each of the six a cappella groups, starting from the top:

  1. Gather the number and colours of Performance dice appearing on the group’s A and B cards (and, if appropriate, its Unexpected Change card) and roll them.
  2. Apply the Venue’s effect to the rolled dice.
  3. If the a cappella group has an Unexpected Change card that changes rolled dice, apply its effect.
  4. Each of the five judges then gives a score to that group:
    • For each die on the judge’s card, count the number of symbols showing on rolled dice of that colour. (If the same colour die appears twice on the judge’s card, double the count of that colour’s symbols.)
    • If the group has an Unexpected Change card that changes judges’ scoring, apply its effect.
    • Record that score, using Hits, in the invisible grid square to the right of the a cappella group cards and below the judge card.
  5. When all five judges have scored the group, combine that group’s Hits into a single overall score for that group.
  6. Tips: Assign each judge to a player, who carries out that judge’s scoring for all six a cappella groups. You can also assign a player to combine the judges’ Hits for a group into the overall score, combining low-duration notes into high-duration notes to free up Hits tokens.

Once all six a cappella groups have been scored by the panel of judges, the group with the highest overall score wins 1st place, and the group with the next highest overall score wins 2nd place!

  • If two or more groups are tied with the same score, the tie is broken in favour of groups that are lowest down in the table. (Recency bias means that the judges might unconsciously favour the last groups to perform.)

Every Content token corresponding to the 1st or 2nd place group earns Hits for the player who possesses it, according to the numbers on the Content token. Each Share token on a Content token earns Hits for the player whose Share token it is, as if that player also possessed the Content token.
